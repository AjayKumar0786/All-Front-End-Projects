What's a VIN Number?

A VIN is a unique code that identifies every vehicle. It can be used to get the most important information about the vehicle. A detailed VIN decode will reveal a piece of every critical information about a vehicle, which can then be used to help you decide whether a car is worth purchasing or not.

Every VIN code is different, which is why it's such a valuable number to use, and each car is assigned its own VIN number when it is manufactured, so you can track the car's history from the moment it left the factory!

Where to find VIN On a vehicle?

So, now that you know what a VIN number is, you're probably wondering where to find it on a vehicle. After all, if you don't know where to find the VIN number, you don't know what the VIN is!

The VIN number is almost always located on the lower-left-hand corner of your dashboard. You'll find it in front of the steering wheel. That means that the easiest way to read the number is through the windshield on the driver's side of your car.

If you're not able to find the VIN number there, don't panic. You can also find it in a few other locations, such as on the engine block.
